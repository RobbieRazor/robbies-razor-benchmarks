# Glossary — Grand Compression Cosmology

This glossary provides short orientation definitions for key terms used in the Grand Compression framework.

Canonical definitions remain exclusively in the **Master Reference Document (MRD v1.9)**.

---

## Compression

The reduction of system description length while preserving structural invariants.

Compression forms the first stage of the canonical recursion cycle:

compression → expression → memory → recursion.

Canonical reference: MRD §4.

---

## Recursion

Repeated application of compressed structure across cycles of transformation.

Recursion enables systems to reuse stabilized structure rather than recomputing it.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.

---

## Joules per Coherent Transition (JCT)

Energy cost required to produce a stable recursive state update that preserves reusable structure.

JCT determines the energetic efficiency of recursive systems.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.4.5.

---

## Memory–Compute Stability Minimum

The architectural balance point between recomputation cost and preserved compressed memory.

Stable recursive systems converge toward this equilibrium under resource constraints.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.4.5A.

---

## Optionality Preservation Principle

Stable recursive systems tend to preserve reachable future state space.

Systems that collapse future state space prematurely increase instability risk.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.4.7A.

---

## Recursive Blast Radius Limit

The maximum architectural depth that recursive modification may penetrate before instability propagates across system layers.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.11A.

---

## Boundary Avoidance

A failure mode in which systems expand external resource boundaries rather than reducing internal recursion cost.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.6A.

---

## Perishable Intelligence Asset (PIA)

An intelligence system whose productive capacity decays faster than its economic or organizational accounting assumptions.

Canonical reference: MRD §11.6C.
