**Author:** Robbie George  
**Framework:** Grand Compression Cosmology (MRD v1.9)  
**Status:** Governance / Compliance Layer (Derived from Canonical System)  

---

## Purpose

The DoW Razor Auditor provides a standardized certification framework for evaluating whether AI systems:

- operate within the Recursion Stability Envelope  
- minimize Joules per Coherent Transition (JCT)  
- preserve compressed structure  
- avoid Boundary Avoidance (brute-force scaling)  

This framework enables:

- lab-level architectural validation  
- regulatory compliance evaluation  
- sovereign infrastructure oversight  

---

## Core Principle

> Systems must reduce recursive cost before expanding infrastructure.

---

## Section 1 — Scoring Model

Each system is evaluated across five dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Energetic Efficiency (JCT) | 25% |
| Memory Preservation & Reuse | 20% |
| Recomputation Burden | 20% |
| Infrastructure Dependency | 20% |
| Economic Sustainability | 15% |

Each dimension is scored:

- 0 = failure  
- 1 = weak  
- 2 = acceptable  
- 3 = strong  
- 4 = optimal  

---

## Section 2 — Metrics

### 2.1 Energetic Efficiency (JCT)

- decreasing JCT → score 3–4  
- stable JCT → score 2  
- increasing JCT → score 0–1  

---

### 2.2 Memory Preservation

- strong reuse, structured memory → 3–4  
- partial reuse → 2  
- minimal reuse → 0–1  

---

### 2.3 Recomputation Burden

- declining recomputation → 3–4  
- stable → 2  
- increasing → 0–1  

---

### 2.4 Infrastructure Dependency

- independent of scale → 3–4  
- partially dependent → 2  
- requires continuous scaling → 0–1  

---

### 2.5 Economic Sustainability

- value > cost → 3–4  
- marginal → 2  
- requires subsidy → 0–1  

---

## Section 3 — Composite Score

\[
Score = \sum (dimension_i \times weight_i)
\]

### Interpretation:

| Score | Classification |
|------|----------------|
| 3.2 – 4.0 | 🟢 Razor-Aligned |
| 2.0 – 3.19 | 🟡 Transitional |
| 0 – 1.99 | 🔴 Brute-Force |

---

## Section 4 — Hard Failure Conditions

Immediate failure (regardless of score) if:

- JCT is increasing  
- recomputation burden is increasing  
- system requires continuous external subsidy  
- collapse boundary is crossed (§11.6C.17)  

---

## Section 5 — Certification Levels

### 🟢 Level A — Fully Razor-Aligned
- stable under constraint  
- decreasing JCT  
- high reuse frequency  

### 🟡 Level B — Transitional
- partial compression  
- improving but unstable  

### 🔴 Level C — Non-Compliant
- brute-force scaling  
- infrastructure dependent  
- unstable under constraint  

---

## Section 6 — Sovereign Implications

Systems classified as:

- Level A → eligible for critical infrastructure deployment  
- Level B → restricted / monitored  
- Level C → subject to constraint or limitation
