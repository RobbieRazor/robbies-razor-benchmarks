# Razor vs Infrastructure Expansion — Decision Rule

**Author:** Robbie George  
**Framework:** Grand Compression Cosmology (MRD v1.9)  
**Status:** Canonical Doctrine Extension  

---

## Overview

This document defines the decision boundary between:

- Legitimate infrastructure expansion  
- Failed brute-force scaling (Boundary Avoidance)

within recursive intelligence systems.

---

## Core Principle

Infrastructure expansion is valid only after recursive efficiency has been maximized.

> Compression precedes scale.

---

## Decision Variables

- E — available energy per unit time  
- JCT — Joules per Coherent Transition  
- R — recursive transition rate  
- S — stabilization bandwidth  
- C — correction demand  
- B_r — recomputation burden  
- U — reuse frequency  

---

## Legitimate Infrastructure Expansion

Infrastructure expansion is valid when:

- JCT is minimized or stable  
- recomputation burden decreases  
- reuse frequency increases  
- system remains within both ceilings:

R ≤ min(E / JCT, S / C)

---

## Boundary Avoidance (Brute Force)

Infrastructure expansion is invalid when:

- JCT does not decrease  
- recomputation burden increases  
- reuse frequency decreases  
- system requires ongoing external subsidy  

---

## Refined Decision Rule

A system is Razor-aligned if:

- JCT_new ≤ JCT_old  
- AND recomputation burden decreases  
- AND reuse frequency increases  
- AND R_new ≤ min(E_new / JCT_new, S / C)

A system is brute-force if any of the above fail.

---

## Interpretation

- Increasing E expands capacity  
- Reducing JCT improves architecture  

Sustainable systems do both, in that order.

---

## Canonical Statement

> Systems that scale energy before reducing recursive cost postpone constraint but do not resolve it.
