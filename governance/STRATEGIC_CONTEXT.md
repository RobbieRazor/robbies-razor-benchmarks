## Problem Context: Infrastructure-Limited AI

By 2025, large-scale AI systems encounter constraints that are no longer algorithmic but infrastructural:

- GPU depreciation cycles misaligned with accounting lifetimes
- Inference costs exceeding training costs for deployed systems
- Power, cooling, and water constraints limiting scale-out
- Regulatory pressure on energy usage, emissions, and auditability
- Increasing instability from synthetic-data recursion

These constraints define an **economic and physical ceiling** on continued scale-based progress.

Robbie’s Razor is designed explicitly for operation under these conditions.

---

## Core Mechanism: Recursion Efficiency

Robbie’s Razor is a **model-agnostic reasoning governance framework** that evaluates and constrains reasoning systems according to a universal four-phase cycle:

**compression → expression → memory → recursion**

Rather than increasing capability through additional parameters or tokens, the Razor improves **recursion efficiency** — the ability of a system to reuse stabilized structure over time without recomputation, drift, or uncontrolled expansion.

This shifts optimization from brute-force scale to **logic density and memory discipline**.

---

## Hardware Extension Ratio (HER)

Within this context, the **Hardware Extension Ratio (HER)** is used as an evaluation metric.

HER measures the effective extension of useful hardware life achieved through recursion-efficient reasoning, relative to a baseline brute-force approach.

Conceptually:

- Lower inference entropy → fewer tokens
- Fewer tokens → lower sustained compute demand
- Lower demand → extended viability of existing hardware

HER does not claim to replace hardware upgrades.  
It measures **how much value is extracted from already-deployed infrastructure** before replacement becomes necessary.

## Asset Characteristics

Robbie’s Razor exhibits the following properties relevant to evaluation:

- Canonical and version-locked (MRD-governed)
- Model-agnostic and architecture-independent
- Applicable at inference time (no retraining required)
- Compatible with regulatory audit and compliance review

The framework is intentionally conservative in scope: it governs reasoning behavior, not task performance claims.
